The Times - 2017

New product strategy & design

The Times and The Sunday Times had a new digital product strategy created by IDEO. This proposed to fundamentally change customers content consumption and provide a more flexible publishing model for editorial teams.

My role

Work with the existing Experience Design Team to validate the product strategy by evolving a product MVP using a test learn cycle.

Methods

  • Concept design
  • Prototyping
  • UI design
  • Usability testing
  • User Research

Team

  • Product Manager
  • UX Designers
  • Editorial Designers
  • Editorial Lead

Approach

Product strategy to MVP

A rapid design approach identified a series of hypotheses to be taken from the product strategy and inform the initial MVP. This was a complete technical slice solution, connected to News UK’s publishing systems.

Concept design - components were initially scamped before being evolved into a basic visual design.


Concept design & testing

Initial research focused on understanding customers existing behaviour and attitudes towards new navigation, IA and using new feature concepts. Remote moderated testing methods were used to conduct interviews, observe tasks and collect opinions. Using a combination of an initial MVP and concept visual designs as the stimulus.

Remote testing sessions - candidate Skype calls were shown on screen in the testing room & key question boards captured notes as sessions unfolded.

Findings

Findings summarised the participant's reaction to features and answered hypothesis questions derived from the product strategy. The participants projected opinions and actual behaviour were compared and recommendations were made for features to keep and ones that needed improving.
This was presented back to disciplines across the digital news functions in line with a data marketing survey on similar themes. Both were used to influence the feature scope for the next MVP phase.


Research findings report - summary of participants reactions to concepts & key recommendations.


Initial MVP

The second conceptual design phase evolved the initial MVP - refining existing features and adding new ones.
Each concept had a 1-week design sprint to conceive, interaction design and conduct a first-pass visual design, before presenting back to the project team.
Many features had a 24-hour lifecycle - these would be scamped up and placed on a framework that represented the proposed editorial cycle key publishing points throughout a day.


MVP design - low fidelity design approach was used to specify & communicate the design.
