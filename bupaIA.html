New IA for Bupa.co.uk

As part of a digital transformation programme to evolve bupa.co.uk into a
customer-centric ‘one Bupa’ experience.

My role

I was responsible for devising a new high-level information architecture for bupa.co.uk
that works for all customer types.

Methods

  • User interviews
  • Web analytics
  • Concept design
  • Unmoderated testing
  • Focus groups

Team

  • Head of Customer Experience
  • Customer Journey Managers
  • Omniture Consultant

Existing and proposed landing pages


Background

A new information architecture to support the transformation of bupa.co.uk into a customer centric ‘one Bupa’ experience. Embracing a fully data-led approach with customer validation to serve customer needs not single products. To show Bupa as a healthcare partner and navigator not just a health insurer.

Initial goals identified

  • Increase engagement with relevant products and services
  • Reduce the cost of serving customers
  • Maximise re-use and reach of content assets
  • Improve tracking web leads through to conversion

Research

Findings

Leveraged existing secondary research, interviewed and surveyed representative customers to identify user needs for all known customer types.

User needs for each customer type

Working with an Omniture consultant to extract useful insights from web analytics, identifying core journey's taken and understand funnels to points of conversion.

Consumer core journey funnel

A heuristic review of core journeys identified some key issues:

  • Unclear navigation for key / high-level user needs
  • High funnel abandonment
  • Hard to find CTA with weak resonance and clarity
  • Difficulty comparing Bupa service offerings

Opportunities Identified

Opportunities were associated with recommendations and potential outcomes. And then prioritised to fulfil user needs and achieve digital goals.

Opportunities matrix - each opportunity was aassociated with a finding a recommendation and potential outcome.


Concept design

IA Options

Created based on differing hypotheses to validate in customer workshops and online unmoderated testing. With 85 participants representing Consumer and Business customers.

Design approaches and hypothesis

  • Goal based - group experiences around similar customer interactions
  • Customer actions - use customer language to improve relevance and understanding
  • Product based - customers are already consider a Bupa offering

IA option testing results


Chosen option

Using a product-led taxonomy provides an ‘easy to find’ navigation structure, ’task based’ navigation can also be used alongside to provide shortcuts to key journeys and personalised based context i.e. contact information and location services.
