BUPA - 2015

Health assessment experience evolution

Evolution of Bupa's health assessment experience, identifying ‘to be’ and future vision states.

My role

Gain a holistic experience of a customer’s healthcare experience to identify insights to improve it’s product and service offering. And aligning improvements to a new design direction for bupa.co.uk.

Methods

Field studies, web analytics, concept design, prototyping, UI design, usability testing

Team

Customer Journey Manager, Business stakeholders, Offshore Development Team

Outcome

  • Discovery of insights to shape ‘To Be’ and future state visions of the health assessment experience
  • Updated designs included clear pathways for all user types at key stages of their health journey and across digital & human touchpoints

New health assessments landing page


Background

There was no holistic understanding of the customer's healthcare experience, with silo reporting that focused on key parts of the customer’s lifecycle. A recent member survey found that 65% of survey respondents found it difficult to choose a health assessment.

Research

Conducted discovery via web analytics, call centre listening, stakeholder interviews and assessment centre visits to observe and interview customers and health centre teams.

Discoveries

Customer findings - the key differentiator between the main customer types was if they received a health assessment as an employee benefit or were paying for it themselves. This influenced their behaviour and expectations of having a health assessment.


Customer proto personas - 2 main customer types were identified that
had differing behaviours & levels of engagement.

Journey stage: Acquisition

High self funders engagement before an assessment - corporate employees aren't typically involved in choosing their health assessment or booking a visit. and also that assessment information was available on their company's intranet.
Challenges with product comparison & selection - site analytics identified visitors looping around products and section pages accessing the same pages. Call centre listening identified struggles finding and understanding site content, with the helpline fielding specific questions about assessments & centre visits.

Journey stage: Health assessment

Situations were occurring that affected perceived value of an assessment.
Visitors were not prepared for assessments - ignoring dietary requests, not bringing sports clothing and not preparing questions for the assessment team. Visitors had either forgotten or weren’t aware this was required of them.
Post-assessment support is valued, but not always utilised - talking through assessment outcomes with a doctor and ongoing support are rated highly. Though aspects of the post-assessment service are under-utilised, even though it’s a key differentiator between Bupa & competitors.

Experience map highlights - Customer lifecycle pain point themes were broken down for
discoveries to be presented back to various teams.


Design

New IA

Combining a series of recommendations from the wider site redesign and discoveries from health assessment research:

  • Simplified access points aligned to the assessment lifecycle
  • Effective navigation for direct SERP access or via the homepage
  • Prioritised self-funded consumer journeys, as the majority of site visitors

Revised IA - to provide focused entry points for visitors need states & simplified content organisation to remove comprehension & navigation challenges encountered.


Choose an assessment

Assisting site visitors in choosing an appropriate assessment with a simplified approach to comparing options, which included:

  • Revised package names that have meaning and are relevant as a set
  • Surfacing headline attributes: assessment duration, number of tests in total and by category and time spent with the doctor
  • Who the test is suitable for and what you’ll understand from it

Concept design Assessment select & configure - simplified & prominent metadata, alternative discovery mechanisms & basic triage/ finder to identify their assessment needs.
