IN-MARKET REDESIGN

Background

An update to the driver’s companion app had launched with poor customer ratings. Requiring a quick turnaround to fix key issues that had caused poor CSAT and app store reviews. Providing an opportunity for the newly formed central design team to take ownership. Unfortunately, the existing team in the US had disbanded so minimal handover was possible.

The business wanted a quick response to resolve the rating problem, influencing the approach taken and reduced the upfront research and testing conducted.

Triangulating research sources

Firstly, I reviewed survey responses and app reviews to identify the main areas of frustration. Then an expert review of problem areas to form assumptions of possible symptoms. Before leveraging existing secondary research, a recent survey with app users, to understand attitudes and preferences.
Revealing:

  • Low correlation between ‘must-have’ features and actual usage
  • Most valued features typically related to emergency and security
  • Most frequently used features related to convenience and maintenance
  • For many, additional feature engagement occurs once users have used the app for a while

Project goals and objectives

The main goal was to improve the app’s user experience, subsequent objectives were identified with stakeholders when playing back initial research discoveries. I added further recommendations to objectives where appropriate.

Project objectives provided focus for this ‘quick-fix’ phase, connecting user needs and business goals.



Key design challenges

Problem: discoverability of frequently used features

  • Resulting in a drop in feature usage
  • Assumption that features like vehicle status and vehicle health were unintuitive to access from the main screen


Improved by making the summary easier to scan, surfacing more summary information and providing simple access to the whole feature set.


Problem: advanced remote perception poor amongst users

  • Resulting in being responsible for 30% of 1-star app ratings alone
  • Assumption that it uses a poor interaction pattern combined with connection unresponsiveness and that the feature disappears once the trial subscription expires


Improved by partially surfacing remote on main screens to improve discoverability, made accessible via thumb reach to enable easier access to full feature set and adding a disabled state and explanation when expired.


Problem: users struggle to make a service appointment

  • Resulting in many customers calling Toyota customer service and dealerships to book a service
  • Assumption that uses an overly complicated screen flow with multiple steps making the interaction confusing


Improved by reducing steps into one progressive input screen, removing separate screens and guiding user input with modal screen states.


Testing solution ideas

Conducted online unmoderated user tests with a clickable prototype and survey with User Zoom. Prototype included archetypal flows of solutions to identified project objectives, using a mix of existing Toyota app and non-app users.

Future vision concept

Background

Product research identified 5 focus areas where a step change could give the app a clear purpose in the Toyota ecosystem. I was tasked with creating a future vision for 2 of these:

  • Safety & Security keep the user’s vehicle safe and secure at all times, providing assistance in case of emergency
  • Navigation plan user’s road trips and locate their parked vehicle, even in congested parking lots
