TOYOTA, 2023

Driver companion app redesign

Toyota released an update to its driver companion app in North America, in late 2022. Resulting in poor app store reviews and customer satisfaction feedback.

My role

Identify the reasons for poor customer reviews and define a series of updates to improve the experience for in-market.

Methods

Usability testing, mobile design, prototyping

Team

User Researcher, Product Designer, Product Management, Development Team

Outcome

Tested prototype for the app redesign across 2 rounds that achieved:

  • +80% of participants completed user’s top tasks
  • +75% of participants completed upfront high-value journeys for channel handoff
  • App update released in September 2023, upon finishing my contract no customer feedback had been collected


In-market redesign

Background

An update to the driver’s companion app had launched with poor customer ratings. Requiring a quick turnaround to fix key issues that had caused poor CSAT and app store reviews. Providing an opportunity for the newly formed central design team to take ownership. Unfortunately, the original design team in the US had disbanded so minimal handover was possible.

Approach

The business wanted a quick response to resolve the rating problem, influencing the approach taken and reduced the upfront research and testing that could be conducted.


Research

Firstly, I reviewed survey responses and app reviews to identify the main areas of frustration. Then an expert review of problem areas to form assumptions on possible symptoms. Finally, I leveraged existing secondary research, a recent survey with app users, to understand attitudes and preferences.
Revealing:

  • Low correlation between ‘must-have’ features and actual usage
  • Most valued features typically related to emergency and security
  • Most frequently used features related to convenience and maintenance
  • For many additional feature engagement occurs once users have used the app for a while

User survey - key to understanding valued & assumed use of app features

Project goals and objectives

The main goal was to improve the app’s user experience, subsequent objectives were identified with stakeholders when playing back upfront research discoveries. I added further recommendations to objectives where appropriate.

Provided focus for this ‘quick-fix’ phase, connecting user needs and business goals.


Key design challenges

Problem: discoverability of frequently used features

  • Resulting in high frequency Dashboard features encountering low usage
  • Assumption that features like vehicle status and vehicle health were unintuitive to access from the main screen


Improved by: making the summary easier to scan, surfacing more summary information and providing simple access to the whole feature set.


Problem: advanced remote perception amongst users was poor due to the features reliability

  • Responsible for 30% of 1-star app ratings alone
  • Assumption that this is due to a poor interaction pattern, connection unresponsiveness and the feature disappearing once the trial subscription expires


Improved by: partially surfacing remote on main screens to improve discoverability, made accessible via thumb reach to enable easier access to full feature set and adding a disabled state and explanation when expired.


Problem: users struggle to make a service appointment

  • Resulting in many customers calling Toyota customer service or dealerships to book a service
  • Assumption this is due to an overly complicated screen flow with multiple steps making the interaction confusing


Improved by: reducing steps into one progressive input screen, removing separate screen steps and guiding user input with modal screen states.


Testing

Conducted online unmoderated user tests using a clickable prototype and survey via User Zoom. Prototype included archetypal flows of solutions to identified project objectives, using a mix of existing Toyota app and non-app users.

Summary

Following 2 rounds of prototype testing the updated designs were committed to the in-market design system and product squads applied these updates to their respective areas.
