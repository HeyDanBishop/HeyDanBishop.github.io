TOYOTA, 2023

Driver companion app redesign

Toyota released an update to its driver companion app in North America, in late 2022. Resulting in poor app store reviews and customer satisfaction feedback.

My role

Identify the reasons for poor customer reviews and define a series of updates to improve the experience for in-market. Design a North Star best-in-class concept for a series of key features to guide the app’s future evolution.

Methods

  • User research
  • Usability testing
  • Customer surveys
  • Mobile design iOS / Android
  • Prototyping
  • Hypothesis design

Team

  • User Researcher
  • Product Designer
  • Product Management
  • Development Team

Outcome

Resolution of all high-severity experience problems identified and provided a basis for further minor releases of this app version. The first release of the app update was launched in September 2023.
North Star concept provided:

  • Recommendations to evolve feature set for evolving in detailed design
  • Contribution to future vision direction for Toyota Connected Services and companion app

In-market redesign

Research

Collaborated with user research to plan a round of usability testing to explore initial themes identified from the customer surveys. These were then triangulated with usage data.
Key findings that provided focus for the rest of the project:

  • A low correlation between ‘must-have’ features and actual usage
  • The highest valued features typically relate to emergency and security feature areas
  • The highest frequently used features were related more to convenience and maintenance
  • Additional feature engagement typically occurs once users have had the app for a while, though engagement is currently low

Key design challenges

Dashboard, low discoverability of frequently used features
Important features Vehicle Status and Vehicle Health were unintuitive to access resulting in a drop in usage.

Improved by: the summary easier to scan, surfacing more summary information
and providing simple access to feature set.


Advanced Remote, poor perception of feature reliability
Due to poor connectivity, unresponsiveness and disappearing once the trial subscription expires, responsible for 30% of 1-star app ratings alone.

Improved by: display on main screens, accessible via thumb reach, easier access to full feature set
and adding a disabled state and explanation when expired.


Service Appointment, users struggle to make an appointment
An overly complicated screen flow made the interaction confusing, with many steps. This resulted in many customers calling Toyota customer service or dealerships to book a service.

Improved by: reducing steps into one progressive input screen, removing separate
screen steps and guiding user input with modal screen states.


Future vision

Companion app visioning

Product research identified 5 focus areas where a step change could give the app a clear purpose in the Toyota ecosystem.
I was tasked with creating a future vision for 2 of these:

  • Safety & Security - keep the user’s vehicle safe and secure at all times, providing assistance in case of emergency
  • Navigation - plan user’s road trips and locate their parked vehicle, even in congested parking lots
