CLARO WELLBEING - 2021

Financial coaching service launch

Claro's missions is to bridge the financial advice gap in the UK where 66% of UK adults don't have access to financial guidance and support. Financial Coaching is believed to be an accessible alternative regardless of people’s situation.

My role

Establish a service proposition, evolve a product from MVP to launch, build out a small design team and establish an experience design process.

Methods

Value proposition development, service & product prototyping, user interviews, user testing, story mapping, mobile design, UI design

Team

Product Manager, Product Designers, Content Design, Growth Strategist


Outcome

Multi-channel service launch focused on delivering accessible financial coaching alongside saving, investing, education and budgeting features.
3 months after launch, the service achieved:

  • 40,000+ users with an average app rating of 4.3/5
  • £2-3 million invested through the app into Claro's ESG funds
  • Conducted 1.5k coaching sessions

Scaled into B2B offering to be a compensation benefit for employees to provide financial coaching, education and access to tools.


PILOT STAGE

Approach

Initial service offering was defined using existing research findings and conducted a generative empathy mapping exercise with a financial advisor to inform the experience design. These were treated as initial hypotheses and validated as part of the pilot.
The service prototype was created using a Wizard of Oz approach using: Calendly, a cashflow tool trial, Zoom and a Financial Advisor to conduct coaching calls.

Testing a service prototype

Pilot purpose:

  • Create a service prototype to test with target customers
  • Uncover insights on customer value, the propensity to pay expectations and needs for initial customer journey stages
  • Validate assumptions made to inform the experience of the pilot

Service pilot customer journey - comprised a journey to book, prepare and attend a Financial Coaching
session and receive an outcome, a financial plan.


Empathy map - find a Financial Advisor - was a quick approach to capture acquired knowledge from a Financial Advisor about target customers.
Influencing early proposition definition and MVP journey design, with assumptions validated over time.



Findings

New customer segment identified

  • Had already achieved some long-term financial goals (purchasing a property) and now focusing on additional life goals (wealth planning)
  • Their main goal regarding coaching was that they were considering parting ways with their financial advisor due to fees, a percentage of assets each year. Feeling they could make wealth decisions themselves instead of being led
  • Wanting a more ‘casual’ relationship with a financial advisor to help with specific goals when required. To work through their decision, be a second opinion and answer specific questions

    • Going it alone persona - created a persona to easily communicate findings on a new target customer with the wider team and influence the emerging value proposition.

    BETA LAUNCH

    End-to-end journey design and longitudinal study

    User journey from app download, attending a coaching session, to starting a financial goal with Claro. Capturing feedback from 20 beta users via a series of micro-surveys and one-to-one interviews, over a four week period.

Early customer journey - an early version used as a reference to
build the end-to-end journey for beta.


Visual design for beta release - first opportunity to apply new brand and
design system to entire customer journey.

Study findings

  • Some journey stages progress slowly - most users needed time to set up financial goals after a coaching session. Over many weeks whereas the assumption had been this would typically be instantaneous
  • Users had low awareness of Claro products - after coaching sessions, many users didn’t realise they could start financial goals with a Claro product. Even though products were associated with a session. Possibly due to coaches only being able to guide decisions but not decide for them
  • Customise coaching sessions - experienced users already knew about some coaching session topics. Some wanted to complete specific activities separately and focus the session on priorities, or high-value topics such as investing
  • People more likely to progress when a goal was already identified - when a financial goal had already been identified by users they typically had higher engagement levels. Which meant they were more likely to move quickly from an initial coaching session to start to work towards that goal

Brand guidelines - findings from Pilot & Beta phase research contributed to key aspects of the brand guidelines.


Beta findings report - key discoveries & validation points used to evolve user journeys.



POST LAUNCH OPTIMISATION

PROBLEM : Users not preparing for coaching sessions

Post-launch coaching session feedback identified that around a third of users weren’t fully preparing for coaching sessions, and some users weren’t even attending the sessions booked.
Impact:

  • Coaches can’t fully prepare and deliver a complete session plan
  • Customer value for coaching outcomes is lowered
  • Session rescheduling or no attendance impacts coach utilisation rates

Hypothesis

After further analysis we formed a solution hypothesis: If users can reach their first milestone of creating a financial plan. They’ll be more accountable to progress, attend coaching sessions and act upon recommendations.

Journey update working canvas - included competitor design research, existing insights and relevant human behaviour assumptions to frame Ideas.

Concept

Focused on how to engage users through a long journey sequence that request lots of data and covers sensitive topics like personal finance. Main ideas applied:

  • Front-load coaching session preparation - moving it earlier in the journey, mostly before booking a coach
  • Leverage internal triggers - using cues to prompt action based on emotions or existing habits
  • Accommodate different starting points - help to identify financial goals, or if known start working through them
  • Personalised financial plan and rewards - use the endowment effect to increase perceived value of a financial plan

Journey prototype - 2 versions of the onboarding journey were created to explore divergent approaches for interaction models, engagement techniques around financial goals, motivation and reward techniques.


Comparative test

Remote unmoderated testing with 35 candidates across 2 prototypes was conducted to collect data on task performance, SUS (System Usability Score) and survey to capture user motivations and preferences.

Findings

Building a financial plan in a user-preferred order achieved more task completions. Choosing a financial goal that was very important provided motivation to also complete aspects of tthe journey perceived as less relevant.

The majority appreciated creating a financial plan required extensive data capture, but still felt that the journey was too long. With a recommendation to:

  • Shift attitudes from data capture steps to creating a plan
  • Decompose the journey into obvious chunks that aren't just about answering questions.

Candidates were split across the rewards to receive by completing the financial plan.

Candidates earlier in their financial journey obtained more value from using the prototype and were also more engaged. These typically knew less about financial planning or hadn’t identified their goals yet.

Further design iteration - based on test findings.
